SECTION 3: CASHLESS TREATMENT
-----------------------------------------------------------
What is cashless?
  → You don't pay the hospital bill directly. The insurance company
    pays the hospital on your behalf after approving the claim.

How to avail cashless treatment:
  Step 1: Visit the Insurance Desk with your insurance e-card or physical card + valid ID.
  Step 2: Our team will verify your policy and check network status.
  Step 3: We submit a pre-authorization request to your insurance company.
  Step 4: Once approved, you receive cashless treatment without upfront payment.
  Step 5: Any non-covered expenses (co-pay, exclusions) must be paid by you.

Important:
  - Cashless is available ONLY if this hospital is in your insurer's network.
  - Always carry your insurance e-card or physical card.
  - Do not leave the hospital without completing cashless discharge formalities.

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: Can I get cashless treatment here?
  A: Yes, cashless treatment is available if your insurance provider has
     a tie-up with our hospital. Visit the insurance desk with your card.

  Q: What documents do I need for cashless?
  A: Bring your insurance card (physical or e-card) and a valid government ID.

  Q: How long does cashless approval take?
  A: Pre-authorization is typically processed within a few hours for planned
     admissions. Emergency cashless approvals are expedited.

SECTION 4: PRE-AUTHORIZATION
-----------------------------------------------------------
What is pre-authorization?
  → A formal approval from your insurance company BEFORE a planned procedure
    or admission. Without this, cashless may be denied.

When is pre-authorization required?
  - All planned (non-emergency) surgeries
  - Planned hospital admissions
  - High-cost procedures (MRI, CT, chemotherapy, dialysis, etc.)
  - ICU admission (for planned care)

How to apply:
  → Visit the insurance desk at least 48 hours before your procedure.
  → Carry: doctor's prescription, investigation reports, insurance card, valid ID.
  → Our insurance team will submit the request on your behalf.

Timeline:
  - Standard pre-authorization: 24–48 hours
  - Emergency pre-authorization: Expedited — contact Duty Manager

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: Do I need pre-authorization for surgery?
  A: Yes, pre-authorization is mandatory for all planned surgeries.
     Apply at the insurance desk at least 48 hours in advance.

  Q: What if I need emergency surgery — do I still need pre-authorization?
  A: In emergencies, treatment begins immediately. Our team will handle
     the insurance approval on your behalf. Attendant must inform the
     insurance desk or Duty Manager as soon as possible.

  Q: How early should I apply for pre-authorization?
  A: Minimum 48 hours before the planned procedure or admission.

SECTION 5: REIMBURSEMENT CLAIMS
-----------------------------------------------------------
What is reimbursement?
  → If you paid the hospital bill yourself (non-cashless), you can claim
    the amount back from your insurance company.

How to file a reimbursement claim:
  Step 1: Collect all original documents at discharge:
            - Itemized hospital bill
            - Discharge summary
            - Doctor's prescriptions
            - Lab and diagnostic reports
            - Payment receipts
  Step 2: Submit these documents to your insurance company directly,
          OR visit the insurance desk — we can assist with paperwork.
  Step 3: Insurance company will verify and process the claim.
  Step 4: Reimbursement credited to your bank account.

Processing Time:
  → Reimbursement claims are typically processed within 15 working days
    by the insurance company (may vary by provider).

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: I paid cash — can I get reimbursement from my insurance?
  A: Yes. Collect all original bills and reports at discharge and submit
     them to your insurance company. The insurance desk can help you
     with the required paperwork.

  Q: How long does reimbursement take?
  A: Typically 15 working days after submission of complete documents.

  Q: What documents do I need for a reimbursement claim?
  A: Original itemized bill, discharge summary, prescriptions, lab reports,
     and payment receipts.

SECTION 6: WHAT IS AND ISN'T COVERED
-----------------------------------------------------------
GENERALLY COVERED (subject to your policy):
  - In-patient hospitalization (admitted for 24+ hours)
  - Surgeries and procedures
  - ICU charges
  - Diagnostic tests (during admission)
  - Medicines (during admission)
  - Ambulance charges (some policies)
  - Daycare procedures (certain policies)
  - Pre and post-hospitalization expenses (some policies)

GENERALLY NOT COVERED (check your policy):
  - OPD (outpatient) consultations — usually NOT covered
  - Routine health checkups
  - Cosmetic procedures
  - Dental treatment (unless trauma-related)
  - Spectacles, hearing aids, external devices
  - Maternity (if not included in your policy)
  - Pre-existing conditions during waiting period
  - Consumables (some policies exclude gloves, syringes, etc.)

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: Is my OPD visit covered by insurance?
  A: OPD visits are usually not covered by standard health insurance.
     Please check your specific policy for OPD coverage.

  Q: Will my surgery be covered?
  A: Most planned surgeries are covered if the hospital is in your
     insurer's network and pre-authorization is obtained. Check your
     policy for exclusions.

  Q: Is delivery / maternity covered?
  A: Maternity coverage depends on your policy. Some plans include it
     after a waiting period. Please check with your insurance provider.

  Q: Is dental covered?
  A: Standard dental treatment is generally not covered. However,
     dental injuries due to accidents may be covered. Check your policy.

SECTION 7: AYUSHMAN BHARAT (PMJAY) — GOVERNMENT SCHEME
-----------------------------------------------------------
What is it?
  → A Government of India health scheme providing free treatment up to
    ₹5 lakh per family per year to eligible beneficiaries.

Who is eligible?
  → Families listed under the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) database.
  → Beneficiaries with a valid Ayushman card / golden card.

How to avail:
  Step 1: Carry your Ayushman Bharat card or Aadhaar card.
  Step 2: Visit the insurance desk — our PMJAY coordinator will verify eligibility.
  Step 3: If eligible, treatment is fully cashless up to ₹5 lakh.

Coverage:
  - 1,500+ medical packages covered
  - Includes surgery, ICU, medicines, diagnostics during admission
  - Pre and post-hospitalization (up to 3 days before, 15 days after)

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: I have an Ayushman card — can I get free treatment here?
  A: Yes! Carry your Ayushman Bharat card and visit the insurance desk.
     Our team will verify eligibility and arrange cashless treatment.

  Q: How much does Ayushman Bharat cover?
  A: Up to ₹5 lakh per family per year for approved medical packages.

  Q: What if I don't have my Ayushman card with me?
  A: You can verify eligibility using your Aadhaar card. Visit the
     insurance desk and our team will assist you.

SECTION 8: CGHS (CENTRAL GOVERNMENT HEALTH SCHEME)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Who is eligible?
  → Central Government employees, pensioners, and their dependents.

How to avail:
  Step 1: Carry your CGHS card and valid ID.
  Step 2: For planned treatment — obtain referral from CGHS dispensary.
  Step 3: Visit the insurance desk with referral letter and CGHS card.
  Step 4: Cashless treatment processed as per CGHS rates.

Important:
  - CGHS rates apply — there may be a difference between CGHS package
    rate and actual hospital charges (patient must pay the difference).
  - Emergency treatment does not require prior referral.

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: I am a government employee with CGHS — is it accepted here?
  A: Yes, CGHS is accepted. Carry your CGHS card and referral letter
     from your dispensary (except in emergencies).

  Q: Do I need a referral for CGHS?
  A: Yes, for planned treatment a CGHS dispensary referral is required.
     For emergencies, no referral is needed.

-----------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 9: ESI (EMPLOYEE STATE INSURANCE)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Who is eligible?
  → Employees earning below ₹21,000/month (₹25,000 for persons with disability)
    working in registered establishments.

How to avail:
  Step 1: Carry your ESI card and valid ID.
  Step 2: For planned admission — obtain referral from ESI dispensary/hospital.
  Step 3: Visit insurance desk with ESI card and referral.
  Step 4: Treatment arranged as per ESI tie-up.

Patient Query Examples:
  Q: My company provides ESI — is it accepted here?
  A: Yes, ESI is accepted. Carry your ESI card and referral letter.

  Q: Do I need a referral for ESI?
  A: Yes, a referral from the ESI dispensary is required for planned treatment.
     Emergency cases are handled directly.
